Lyapunov Instability Theorem

The system is not asymptotically stable. If P is negative definite, the system is unstable. We know that Q is identity matrix therefore it is positive definite, that mean \(\dot V(x(t))<0\). If P is negative definite, there is \(w\) such that \(V(w)<V(0)\) then the trajectory of \(\dot x\) does not converge to zero. To show it, we note that \(V(x(t))<V(x(0))=V(w)<V(0)=0\) for all \(t\ge0\). That means the system will diverge and be unstable. If P is positive semi-definite, the system is marginally stable but not asymptotically stable. To show that, we know that Q is identity matrix therefore it is positive definite, that mean \(\dot V(x(t))<0\). If P is positive semi-definite, there is \(w\) such that \(V(w)=V(0)\) then the trajectory of \(\dot x\) may converge to w instead of origin. Therefore, the system is marginally stable.

Impulse Response \(g(t)=\sin \omega t\)

The system is not BIBO stable and not asymptotically stable in the sense of Lyapunov.

To show the system is not BIBO stable. we check \(\int_0^\infty|g(t)|dt=\infty\) .If we choose the bounded input according to \[ u(t-\tau)= \begin{cases} 1,&if\space g(\tau)>0\\ 0,&if\space g(\tau)=0\\ -1,&if\space g(\tau)<0\\ \end{cases} \] Then \(|y(t)|=|\int_0^tg(\tau)u(t-\tau)d\tau|=\int_0^t|g(\tau)|d\tau\) and \(\int_0^\infty|g(t)|dt=\infty\) .This means output is unbounded under a bounded input. Therefore, the system is not BIBO stable.

To show the system is not asymptotically stable in the sense of Lyapunov.

If the system is asymptotically stable in the sense of Lyapunov, that implies all eigenvalues of A have negative real parts. On the other hand, BIBO stability is defined for the transfer Matrix of \[ y(s)=G(s)u(s)=[C(sI-A)^{-1}B+D]u(s) \] and poles of G(s) are eigenvalues of A. Hence, all poles of \(G(s)\) have negative real parts and this established that the system is BIBO stable. Since asymptotically stable in the sense of Lyapunov implies BIBO stable. Therefore, from the contrapositive, not BIBO stable implies not asymptotically stable in the sense of Lyapunov.
