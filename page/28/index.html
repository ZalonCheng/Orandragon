Controllable Canonical Form

Proof of existence,

When \(rank(M_c)=n\), then \(M_c^{-1}\) exists. Let \(q^T\) be the nth row of \(M_c^{-1}\)

Then from \(M_c^{-1}M_c=I\) , we have \[ q^TA^iB=0 \quad(i=0,1,2,\dotsm,n-2) \\ q^TA^{n-1}B=1 \] Let the transfer function \[ T=\begin{bmatrix} q^T \\q^TA \\ \vdots \\q^TA^{n-1} \end{bmatrix}\\ \bar B = TB=\begin{bmatrix} 0 \\0\\ \vdots \\1 \end{bmatrix} \\ \bar AT=TA \\ \bar A\begin{bmatrix} q^T \\q^TA \\ \vdots \\q^TA^{n-1} \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} q^T \\q^TA \\ \vdots \\q^TA^{n-1} \end{bmatrix} A =\begin{bmatrix} q^TA \\q^TA^2 \\ \vdots \\q^TA^{n} \end{bmatrix} \] Denote \(\bar A_i\) is the ith line of \(\bar A_i\)

We can observe the above equation and find the solution \[ \bar A_1=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 & 0 & \dotsm & 0 \end{bmatrix} \\ \bar A_2=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & 1 & \dotsm & 0 \end{bmatrix} \\ \bar A_i=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 & \overbrace{1}^{i} & \dotsm & 0 \end{bmatrix} \] Then we need to solve the last line

According to Caley-Hamilton Equation \[ A^n=-\sum_{i=0}^{n-1}a_iA^i \] Therefore, \[ \bar A_n=\begin{bmatrix} -a_0 & -a_1 & -a_2 & \dotsm & -a_{n-1} \end{bmatrix}\\ \] Q.E.D.

Example \[ \dot x=Ax+Bu \\ A= \begin{bmatrix}-2 & -3 \\ -4 & -5 \end{bmatrix} B= \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \\ M_c= \begin{bmatrix}B & AB \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1 & -8\\ 2 & -14 \end{bmatrix} \\ M_c^{-1}=\begin{bmatrix}-7 & 4 \\ -1 & 0.5\end{bmatrix} \\ q^T=[-1 \quad 0.5]\\ T=\begin{bmatrix} q^T \\q^TA \\ \vdots \\q^TA^{n-1} \end{bmatrix}=\begin{bmatrix}-1 & 0.5 \\ 0 & 0.5\end{bmatrix}\\ \bar B = TB = \begin{bmatrix}0 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} \\ \bar A= TAT^{-1} = \begin{bmatrix}0 & 1 \\ 2 & -7\end{bmatrix} \] We get the Controllable Canonical Form.

Observable Canonical Form

\(w\) is the last column of observability matrix inverse \(M_o^{-1}\) \[ M_o=\begin{bmatrix} C\\ CA\\ \vdots\\ CA^{n-1} \end{bmatrix}\\ Q=[w \quad Aw \quad \dotsm \quad A^{n-1}w] \\ \bar A = Q^{-1}AQ \\ \bar B = Q^{-1}B \\ \bar C=CQ \] Proof is similar to the previous

Example \[ \dot x=Ax+Bu \\ A= \begin{bmatrix}-2 & -3 \\ -4 & -5 \end{bmatrix} B= \begin{bmatrix}1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \\ C= \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \\ M_o= \begin{bmatrix}C \\CA \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}1&1 \\ -6 & -8 \end{bmatrix} \\ M_o^{-1}= \begin{bmatrix}4 & 0.5 \\ -3& -0.5 \end{bmatrix} \\ w=\begin{bmatrix}0.5 \\ -0.5 \end{bmatrix} \\ Q=[w \quad Aw \quad \dotsm \quad A^{n-1}w]=\begin{bmatrix}0.5 & 0.5 \\-0.5 & 0.5\end{bmatrix} \\ \bar A= Q^{-1}AQ=\begin{bmatrix}0 & 2 \\1 & -7\end{bmatrix}\\ \bar B= Q^{-1}B=\begin{bmatrix}-1 \\3\end{bmatrix}\\ \bar C=CQ=[0 \quad 1] \]

1. Input-Output Stability

==Theorem 1==

A SISO system is BIBO stable if and only if \[ \int_0^\infty|g(t)|dt \le k\le\infty \] for some constant k.

==Theorem 2==

A transfer function G(s) is BIBO stable if and only if all poles of G(s) are in the open left-half s-plane.

Equivalently, all poles of G(s) have negative real parts.

3. Lyapunov Theorem

==Theorem 3==

  1. V(x) is continuous in domain D
  2. V(0)=0
  3. V(0)>0 except at x=0
  4. \(\frac{dV}{dt}\le0\) stable
  5. \(\frac{dV}{dt} < 0\) asymptotically stable

==Theorem 4==

Given \(\dot x=Ax\) .The system is said to be stable in the sense of Lyapunov (i.s.L.) if and only if all eigenvalues of A have zero or negative real parts and those with zero real parts has no Jordan block of order 2 or higher.

The system is asymptotically stable if all the eigenvalues of A have negative real parts.

==Theorem 5==

Asymptotically stable i.s.L. implies BIBO stability

==Theorem 6==

The linear system \[ \dot x(t)=Ax \] is asymptotically stable if and only if for any Q which is positive definite and symmetric, there exists a P which is symmetric and positive definite satisfying the following equation \[ A^TP+PA=-Q \] known as the Lyapunov equation.

==Example== \[ \dot x= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}x \\ \] We choose \(Q=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\) . Then we have \[ A^TP+PA=-Q \\ \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} p_{11} & p_{12} \\ p_{21} & p_{22} \end{bmatrix}+ \begin{bmatrix} p_{11} & p_{12} \\ p_{21} & p_{22} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ -1 & -1 \end{bmatrix}=-\begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] We get \[ P=\begin{bmatrix} 1.5 & 0.5 \\ 0.5 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \] Therefore, the system is asymptotically stable.

4. L stability

Define \(\mathcal L_1\) \[ ||u(t)||_1=\int_0^\infty|u_i(t)|dt \\ \] For MIMO \[ ||u(t)||_1=\sum_{i=1}^m||u_i(t)||_1 \] Define \(\mathcal L_2\) \[ ||u(t)||_2=\sqrt{\int_0^\infty u(t)^Tu(t)dt} \] Define\(\mathcal L_\infty\)
\[ ||u(t)||_\infty=max||u(t)|| \] The system can be seen as a mapping in the form of \(y=Su\)

Then the system norm is \[ ||S||_p=sup\frac{||y||_p}{||u||_p}=sup\frac{||Su||_p}{||u||_p} \] ==Definition==

A system with input-output model S is \(\mathcal L_p\) stable if

and only if its p induced norm is finite. Particularly, a system

is BIBO stable if and only if it is \(\mathcal L_\infty\) stable.

For the MIMO (m-input-q-output) system \[ ||S||_\infty=\max_{1\le i\le q}\sum_{1\le j\le m}\int_0^\infty|H_{ij}(t)|dt \] For the SISO system \[ ||S||_\infty=\int_0^\infty|h(t)|dt \]

==\(\mathcal{H}_\infty\)==

\[ ||S||_2<max_{w\in R}||H(w)||_2 \]

\(max_{w\in R}||H(w)||_2\) is known as \(\mathcal H_\infty\) norm of system S

We define \[ M_\gamma=\begin{bmatrix} A & \frac{1}{\gamma^2}BB^T\\ -C^TC & -A^T \end{bmatrix} \] then \(\mathcal H_\infty < \gamma\) if and only if \(M_\gamma\) has no purely imaginary eigenvalues.

==\(\mathcal{H}_2\)==

\(\frac{1}{2\pi}Tr\int_{-\infty}^\infty H^T(w)H(w)dw​\) is known as \(\mathcal H_2​\) norm of system S \[ \mathcal H_2^2=Tr(CWC^T)=Tr(B^TMB) \\ \text{W is Controllability Grammain}\\ \text{M is Observability Grammain}\\ AW+WA^T+BB^T=0 \\ A^TM+MA+C^TC=0 \]

==Relationship==

If one system is asymptotically stable, it must be BIBO stable.

If one system is not asymptotically stable, it can be BIBO stable.

Example \[ \dot x=Ax+Bu \\ y=Cx\\ A=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 2\\ 1 & -1\\ \end{bmatrix} B=\begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix} C=\begin{bmatrix} 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix}\\ |\lambda I-A|=\begin{vmatrix} \lambda & -2\\ -1 & \lambda+1 \end{vmatrix}=\lambda(\lambda+1)-2=0\\ \lambda_1=1 \\ \lambda_2=-2\\ \] The system is not asymptotically stable.

However, the transfer function are \[ C(SI-A)^{-1}B=\frac{s-1}{(s+2)(s-1)} \] Which is BIBO stable.
