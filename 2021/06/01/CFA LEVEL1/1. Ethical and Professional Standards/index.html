1. Ethical and Professional Standards

Reading 1 Ethical and Professional Standards

1. Ethics

  • Key words
    • Guiding beliefs or ideals
    • Moral principles
  • Ethical conduct
    • Follows the moral principal
    • Balances the self-interest

2. Code of Ethics

  • Definition
    • Codify the obligatory and forbidden conduct
  • Role
    • General guidance

3. Standard of Conduct

  • Expand the code of ethics
  • Provide the explicit the rule or standards
  • Benchmark for the minimally acceptable behavior

4. Professionalism

  • Explain
    • Specific education
    • Practice underpins trust, respect and recognition
  • Goal
    • Establish the trust among clients and society

5. Challenge to Ethic Conduct

  • Two aspects:
    • Over-confidence
    • Situational influence
      • Money
      • Promotions
      • Prestige
      • Loyalty (most powerful)
      • Compliance culture

6. Ethical and Legal Standards

  • Legal and ethical conduct are not always the same

  • Law is not always the best mechanism

    • Slow
    • New law may be vague
    • Law varies from countries
    • Law subjects to interpretation

7. Ethical Decision-Making Framework

  • Identify
    • Relevant facts
    • Owned duties
    • Ethical principles
    • Conflict of interest
  • Consider:
    • Situational influences
    • Additional guidance
    • Alternative actions
  • Decide and act
  • Reflect

Reading 2 Code of Ethics and Standards of Professional Conduct

1. Proceedings

Primary principles

  • Fairness
  • Confidentiality

PCP and PCS

  • Professional conduct program (PCP)
    • Professional conduct statements (PCS)

Board of Governors、DRC、SPC

  • Board of Governors
    • Maintains oversight and responsibility of PCP
  • Discipline Review Committee (DRC)
    • Responsible for enforcement of PCP
  • Standard of Practice Council (SPC)
    • Maintenance and interpret standards

Source

  • Self-disclosure on annual PCS
  • Written complaints
  • Public sources
  • Violation during examination from exam proctor

Work flow

  • Find a violation
  • Proposes a sanction
    • Accept
    • Reject, referred to a panel composed of DRC members (must) and local members (not must)

Sanctions

  • Public Censure
  • Suspension
  • Revocation

Members of CFA institute must

  • Act with integrity, competence, diligence, respect and in an ethical manner (CREDIT) with public, clients, prospective clients, employers, employees, colleagues in the investment profession, and other participants in the global capital markets.

1. Professionalism

  1. knowledge of Law

    • 知法：知道相关法律

    • 守法：遵守最严格法律和CFA Standard

    • 违法：

      • 发现违规，内部举报，若不成功，隔离自己或者离职
      • 怀疑违规，咨询他人，自我判断
      • 不作为违规

  2. Independence and Objectivity

    • 礼物

      • 拒绝所有礼物，除非是 Modest and normal gift
        • Token，水杯，笔
      • 业绩完成后可以收礼物，要Disclosure

    • Buy-side基金经理不可以施压sell-side分析师

      IBD和Research Analyst之间要有防火墙

      • 直属boss不能相同

      • 业绩不能相互挂钩，可以挂钩全公司业绩

      • 位置隔离

    • 找第三方机构要独立客观（外部分析师、基金经理等）

    • Issuer-paid research可以，但是只能收Flat fee，且需要披露

    • 差旅费自己出，除非无法达到

  3. Misrepresentation

    • 及时改错

    • 不能胡说八道

    • 正确引用

  4. Misconduct

    • 不做欺诈盗窃
    • 不能工作酗酒
    • 例外：非欺诈造成的个人破产免责

2. Integrity of Capital Markets

  1. Material Nonpublic information (MNI)

    • Definition

      • Material
        • Reliable source
        • Clear impact
      • Nonpublic
        • 公开在付费网站算公开

    • 可以使用非公开非重大消息 (Mosaic Theory)

    • 如果market maker知道内幕消息，被动作市

    • 如果risk-arbitrage trading知道内幕消息，best to stop，除非证明内控有效

  2. Market Manipulation

    • Info-based
    • Transaction-based
    • Exception:
      • 期货交易所满足最低交易量与会员达成的协议
      • 为节税
      • 符合交易策略

3. Duty to client

  1. Loyalty, prudence and care

    • Fiduciary duty 受托责任
      • 受托人对于委托人的责任
    • 客户
      • Individual
      • Beneficiary
        • 受益人 (Pension, Trust)
      • Mandate
        • 投资策略
      • Investing public
    • Soft-dollar
      • 受委托公司给的回扣服务，要直接用于客户
    • Directed brokerage
      • 先找best practice再看best price，客户坚持指定劝说失败要written consent
    • Proxy voting policy
      • 必须有，基于性价比考虑是否参与

  2. Fair dealing 客户与客户之间

    • VIP可以但是不能损伤其他客户

    • Trade on order-size

    • Family client 和 Immediate family

      • Family client:

        • Fee-paying: client

        • None fee-paying: 自己账户

        • Beneficiary: 自己账户

      • Immediate family client : 自己账户

  3. Suitability

    • IPS 每年更新一次
    • 重大事件修改IPS
    • 若客户固执，若影响不大可执行，若大则先改IPS，不改则剥离资产或放弃客户

  4. Performance presentation (若违反，则违反Professionalisms misrepresentation)

    • 过去不等于未来，要全面包含不能剔除
    • 业绩简报可以，但必须提供详细信息
    • Composite不同不能放一起

  5. Preservation of confidentiality

    • 例外：违法，法律要求，客户允许

4. Duty to Employers

  1. Loyalty
    • 在职：
      • 不与雇主利益发生冲突除非许可（注册公司可以，不能营业）
    • 离职：
      • 离职前不拿雇主一针一线，除非许可
      • 客户名单只可以有名字，不能有任何其他的，可以通过公开方式找联系方式
      • 仅联系部分客户可以
      • 如果有non-compete agreement则不可以联系老客户或卖产品
  2. Additional compensation arrangement
    • 各方面同意可以拿
  3. Responsibility of supervisors
    • 事前：
      • 下属违规则上司违规，除非尽责
      • 接受岗位前必须确保合规，否则不能接受
    • 事后：
      • 发现违规启动调查
      • 限制当事人

5. Investment

  1. Diligence and reasonable basis
    • 报告全面，top-down, bottom-up
    • 第三方报告或模型要尽职调查
    • 用量化模型要熟悉
    • 团队报告不同意仍署名，但不可私下分享idea
  2. Communication with clients
    • 内容：
      • 任何重大改变告诉客户
    • 形式：
      • 简报+详细信息
    • 注意事项：
      • 观点不等于事实
      • 观点不能与公司反向
  3. Record retention
    • 纸质或电子
    • 有规定服从规定，无规定则保存7年
    • 可以用公开信息重新生成记录

6. Conflicts of Interest

  1. Disclosure of conflict
    • Personal trading 和 board membership -> employer和client
    • corporate financing 和 market relationship -> client
    • 重大个人关系 -> client
  2. Priority of transaction
    • Client>employer>individual （窗口期要足够长，隔一天不行，要一周）
    • Aligned interest：客户让经理一起买的产品，可以跟客户一起交易
  3. Referral fees
    • Disclosure

7. Responsibility as members

  1. Conduct as members and candidates
    • 不能讨论考试内容
  2. Reference to CFA institute, designation
    • 不能预期时间
    • CFA不能加粗加大使用，要和名字一样大
    • 持续使用每年写PCS，付钱
    • 不可以over promise
    • 如果每个考试都是一次通过可以写

Reading 4, 5 Introduction to GIPS

  1. 公司遵守GIPS，真实管理资产的公司（中国银行资管部也算一个公司）
  2. 不可以部分遵守，必须全公司遵守，并且遵守全部条文
  3. Firm Assets
    • Discretionary
      • Fee-paying 必须放composite
      • None Fee-paying 可以放composite （例如公益基金）
    • Non-discretionary一定不能放composite展示
  4. Verification
    • 第三方机构，该机构可以不遵守GIPS，也可以不管理资产（一般是会计公司）
    • 验证是自愿的，若不验证也可以宣称符合GIPS
  5. 宣称遵守GIPS展示前5年业绩，若向前不到5年，则从营业开始，接下来每一年总计展示10年业绩
  6. GIPS 共有 9 部分内容
    1. Fundamental of compliance
    2. Input Data
    3. Calculation Methodology
    4. Composite Construction
    5. Disclosure
    6. Presentation and Reporting
    7. Real Estate
    8. Private Equity
    9. Wrap Fee/Separately Managed Account Portfolio
  7. 用Fair Value进行估值
    • Fair value估值顺序
      • market price
      • market price for similar investment
      • market-based inputs for pricing the investment
      • subjective unobservable inputs for pricing the investment

错题

  1. Ethical conduct is the behavior that is perceived to be beneficial as per society’s ethical expectations.

  2. An investment fund manager has a finance degree and over 20 years of experience working for a top-ranking asset management firm. Based only on this information, could the investment fund manager most likely claim to be part of a profession? No, unless adhere to the code of ethics.

  3. Ensuring that a country’s interests are taken into account when effectively implementing the GIPS standards on a country-wide basis most likely relies on a local sponsoring organization.

  4. Can an individual most likely cause the public to lose confidence in the global financial markets? Yes.

  5. Under what circumstances could a client possibly win a lawsuit against a financial adviser despite the financial adviser abiding by all regulatory and legal requirements? The adviser violating his employer’s published code of ethics.

  6. Gregor Pavlov, CFA, is a fund manager working for the general partner of a new private equity fund. Pavlov includes in the fund marketing material his performance history from his previous employer. He received permission from his former employer to take his historical recommendations and the supporting research reports he used to make those recommendations. Did Pavlov most likely violate the CFA Institute Standards? Yes, with regard to Record Retention.

  7. According the GIPS standards, for periods beginning on or after 1 January 2011, the aggregate fair value of total firm assets most likely includes all: fee- and non–fee-paying discretionary and non-discretionary accounts.

  8. Disclosure of confidential CFA exam information will most likely be detected by the Professional Conduct staff through: monitoring online and social media.

  9. While waiting in the business class lounge before boarding an airplane, Becca Msafari, CFA, an equity analyst, overhears a conversation by a group of senior managers, including members of the Board, from a large publicly listed bank. The managers discuss staff changes necessary to accommodate their regional expansion plans. Msafari hears several staff names mentioned. Under what circumstances could Msafari most likely use this information when making an investment recommendation to her clients? If the discussed changes are unlikely to affect investor perception of the bank.

  10. Lawrence Hall, CFA, and Nancy Bishop, CFA, began a joint research report on Stamper Corporation. Bishop visited Stamper’s corporate headquarters for several days and met with all company officers. Prior to the completion of the report, Bishop was reassigned to another project. Hall utilized his and Bishop’s research to write the report but did not include Bishop’s name on the report because he did not agree with and changed Bishop’s conclusion included in the final report. According to the CFA Institute Standards of Practice Handbook, did Hall most likely violate any CFA Institute Standards of Professional Conduct? No.

  11. Firms claiming GIPS compliance must make every reasonable effort to provide a compliant presentation to which of the following? Prospective clients (No Existing clients, However, it is a recommendation that existing clients receive at least annually a compliant presentation.)

  12. Jeffrey Jones passed the Level I CFA examination in 1997 and the Level II examination in 2009. He is not currently enrolled for the Level III examination. According to the CFA Institute Standards of Professional Conduct, which of the following is the most appropriate way for Jones to refer to his participation in the CFA Program?

    Jeffrey Jones, CFA (expected 2014)

    Candidate in the CFA Institute CFA Program

    Passed Level II of the CFA examination in 2009

  13. CFA Institute Bylaws and Rules of Procedure form the basic structure for enforcement of the Code and Standards.

  14. Paper was recently terminated as one of a team of five managers of an equity fund. The fund had two value-focused managers and terminated one of them to reduce costs. In a letter sent to prospective employers, Paper presents, with written permission of the firm, the performance history of the fund to demonstrate his past success.

    Paper did not violate the Code and Standards.

    Paper violated the Code and Standards by claiming the performance of the entire fund as his own.

    Paper violated the Code and Standards by including the historical results of his prior employer.

  15. Situational influences in decision making will most likely be minimized if: longer-term consequences are considered.

  16. Decision makers who use a compliance approach are most likely to: oversimplify decision making.

    A compliance approach can oversimplify decision making and may not encourage decision makers to consider the larger picture. A strong compliance culture may be a good start in developing an ethical culture but can become another situational influence that may result in employees failing to consider other important factors.

  17. When unethical behavior erodes trust in an investment firm, that firm is more likely to experience: lower revenues and higher expenses.

    Unethical behavior ultimately harms investment firms. Clients are not attracted if they suspect unethical behavior, leading to less business and lower revenues. Investment firms may also experience higher relative costs because regulators are more likely to have cause to initiate costly investigations.

  18. As stated in the revised 11th edition, the Standards of Professional Conduct: address the risks and limitations of recommendations being made to clients.
