Proof of Lyapunov Stability

Proof Lyapunov Theorem for Local Stability

Let us think about the simple case n=2.

To show the stability, we must show that given any strictly positive number R, there exists a (smaller) strictly positive number r such that any trajectory starting inside the ball \(B_r\) remains inside the ball \(B_R\) for all future time. We show this in mathematical language. \[ \forall R>0, \exist r>0,||x(0)||<r\implies \forall t \ge0, ||x(t)||<R \] Now we will prove it.

Let m be the minimum of V on the sphere \(S_R\) , since V is continuous and positive definite, m exists and definitely positive.

Since \(V(0)=0\), therefore we have a smaller ball \(B_r\) around origin such that \(V(x)<m\) for any x inside this ball, here m is the minimum of V on the sphere \(S_R\) .

Now consider one point starts inside the ball \(B_r\) . Since V is non-increasing along system trajectories, V remain strictly smaller than m and therefore it will never cross the the sphere \(S_R\) since m is the minimum of V on the sphere \(S_R\) . If the trajectory cross \(S_R\), there will be one point of V greater than m which contradicts with the condition, \(\dot V(x)\) is negative semi-definite. Therefore Lyapunov stability is guaranteed.

Now we assume that \(\dot V(x)\) is negative definite, and show asymptotic stability, by contradiction.

We suppose the trajectory \(x(t)\) does not converge to zero, that means \(V(x)\) is not converged to zero. But we know \(V(x)\) is non-negative and decreasing, therefore it converges to one scalar \(\epsilon\) . We have \[ \epsilon \le V(x(t))\le V(x(0)) \] Then we can say \[ -\gamma=\max_{\alpha\le||x||\le \beta} \dot V(x) \] Clearly \(-\gamma<0\) since \(\dot V(x)​\) is locally negative definite, then we have \[ V(x(t))=V(x(0)+\int_0^t\dot V(x(\tau))d\tau\le V(x(0))-\gamma t \] If t is greater and greater, then result in contradiction \(V(x(t))\) is positive definite.

Q.E.D
