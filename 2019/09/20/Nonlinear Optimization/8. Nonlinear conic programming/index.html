8. Nonlinear Conic Programming

We consider the following optimization problem, \[ (OP)\quad \begin{array}{rCl} \min & f(x)\\ \text{subject to}& h(x)=0\\ &g(x)\in \mathcal C, \end{array} \] where \(f:\mathcal X\rightarrow \mathbb R,h:\mathcal X\rightarrow \mathbb R^m\), and \(g:\mathcal X\rightarrow \mathcal Z\) are continuously differentiable, \(\mathcal X\) and \(\mathcal Z\) are finite dimensional Euclidean spaces each equipped with a scalar product \(\langle \cdot,\cdot\rangle\) and its induce norm \(||\cdot||\), and \(\mathcal C\) is a closed convex set in \(\mathcal Z\).

If we have \(\mathcal X=\mathbb R^n\), \(\mathcal Z=\mathbb R^q\), and \(\mathcal C=\mathbb R^q_+\). In this case, the problem reduces to the following, \[ (NLP)\quad \begin{array}{rCl} \min & f(x)\\ \text{subject to}& h(x)=0\\ &g(x)\ge 0. \end{array} \] Example

If \(\mathcal X=\mathbb S^n\), \(f(X)=\langle C,X\rangle,\) \(h(X)=\mathcal A(X)-b\), \(g(X)=X\) and \(C=\mathbb S_+^n\), then the problem (OP) reduces to the following linear semidefinite programming problem, \[ (NLP)\quad \begin{array}{rCl} \min & \langle C,X\rangle\\ \text{subject to}& \mathcal A(X)-b=0\\ &X\succeq 0(X\in\mathbb S^n_+), \end{array} \] where \(C\in \mathbb S^n\), \(\mathcal A:\mathbb S^n\rightarrow \mathbb R^m\) is a linear mapping defined by \[ \mathcal A(X)= \begin{bmatrix} \langle A_1,X\rangle \\ \vdots \\ \langle A_m,X\rangle \end{bmatrix},\quad X\in\mathbb S^n, \]

where \(A_k\in \mathbb S^n,k=1,\dotsm,m\), are the constraints matrices. Let \(\mathcal A^*:\mathbb R^m\rightarrow \mathbb S^n\) be the adjoint of \(\mathcal A\) defined by \[ \mathcal A^*y=\sum_{k=1}^m y_kA_k,\quad y\in \mathbb R^m. \] This can be shown by the definition of the adjoint of operator. \[ \begin{array}{rcl} \langle \mathcal A^* y,X\rangle &=&\langle y,\mathcal A X\rangle\\ &=&\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^m y_i\langle A_i,X\rangle \\ &=&\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^m y_i\sum_{j=1}^ne_j^TA_iXe_j \\ \bold 1^T\mathcal A^*yX \bold1 &=& \displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^m y_i \bold1^TA_iX\bold1\\ &=& \bold1^T\displaystyle\sum_{i=1}^m y_i A_iX\bold1, \end{array} \] Therefore, we have \(\mathcal A^*y=\sum_{i=1}^m y_iA_i\).

We have Lyapunov function, \[ \begin{array}{rcl} L(X,y,Z)&=&\langle C,X\rangle +\langle y,b-\mathcal A(X) \rangle+\langle Z,-X\rangle\\ &=&\langle y,b\rangle+\langle C-\mathcal A^*y-Z,X\rangle\quad X\in\mathbb S^n,y\in\mathbb R^n,Z\in\mathbb S^n. \end{array} \] Then \[ \begin{array}{rcl} \theta(y,Z)&=&\displaystyle \inf_{X\in\mathbb S^n} \left\{\langle y,b\rangle+\langle C-\mathcal A^*y-Z,X\rangle\right\}\\ &=&\displaystyle\inf_{X\in\mathbb S^n} \left\{\langle y,b\rangle+\langle C-\mathcal A^*y-Z,X\rangle\right\}\\ &=& \begin{cases} \langle y,b\rangle&\text{if }C-\mathcal A^*y-Z=0\\ -\infty & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases} \end{array} \] Therefore, the dual problem is given by, \[ (D)\quad \begin{array}{rCl} \min & \langle y,b\rangle\\ \text{subject to}& C-\mathcal A^*y-Z=0\\ &Z\succeq 0. \end{array} \]
