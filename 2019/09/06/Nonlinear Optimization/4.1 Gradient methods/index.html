Gradient methods

4.1 Gradient methods for unconstrained optimization problem

Consider the multidimensional unconstrained minimization problem \[ \min f(x)\\ s.t. x\in S \] In the situation where \(\nabla f(x)=0\) cannot be solved analytically, we look for an approximate solution via iterative methods.

Descent property At a given point \(x^*\), let \(\hat d=-\frac{\nabla f(x^*)}{||\nabla f(x^*)||}\). The value of \(f\) decreases most rapidly along the unit direction \(\hat d\) and the rate of change of \(f\) at \(x^*\) along the direction \(\hat d\) is \(-||\nabla f(x^*)||\).

In general, a direction \(d\) such that \(\langle \nabla f(x^*),d\rangle <0\) is called a descent direction.

The direction \(-\nabla f(x^*)\) is known as the steepest descent direction since it gives the fastest rate of decrease in \(f(x)\) among all directions.

Remark The most difficult part of the steepest descent method usually is to find the \(t_k\) that minimizes \(f\) along the gradient direction.
